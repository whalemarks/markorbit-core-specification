# Purpose

Knowledge alone is not enough.

Professional work requires judgment.

The purpose of Intelligence is to continuously transform professional knowledge into professional judgment.

Intelligence answers one question.

> **What should happen next?**

---

# Intelligence Philosophy

Reality exists.

Data describes reality.

Brain explains reality.

Intelligence evaluates reality.

Professional judgment combines:

- facts
- knowledge
- context

Judgment should support decisions.

It should not replace professional responsibility.

---

# The Judgment Pipeline

Intelligence continuously transforms knowledge into judgment.

```
Knowledge Products

↓

Signals

↓

Reasoning

↓

Intelligence Objects

↓

Feedback

↓

Intelligence Products
```

Each layer has one responsibility.

---

# Knowledge Products

Intelligence begins with knowledge.

Knowledge Products include:

- Wiki
- Rules
- Decisions
- Best Practices
- Knowledge Graph

Without knowledge there can be no professional judgment.

---

# Signals

Signals indicate that attention may be required.

Signals originate from many places.

Examples include:

Data

- status changes
- timeline events
- relationship changes

Workspace

- customer behavior
- business activity
- manual input

External

- legal changes
- policy updates
- market events

Signals are observations.

Signals are not conclusions.

---

# Reasoning

Reasoning combines signals with knowledge.

Reasoning may include:

- rule reasoning
- similarity reasoning
- historical reasoning
- statistical reasoning
- AI-assisted reasoning

Reasoning should remain explainable whenever practical.

Professional transparency is more important than automation.

---

# Intelligence Objects

Reasoning produces reusable professional judgments.

Examples include:

- Recommendation
- Risk
- Alert
- Insight
- Prediction
- Priority

Intelligence Objects never execute work.

They support professional decisions.

---

# Recommendation

Recommendations suggest possible actions.

Examples include:

- Recommended Country
- Recommended Class
- Recommended Goods
- Recommended Filing Strategy
- Recommended Attorney

Recommendations should explain why they exist.

---

# Risk

Risk evaluates uncertainty.

Examples include:

- Registration Risk
- Refusal Risk
- Opposition Risk
- Cancellation Risk
- Non-use Risk

Every Risk should include:

- explanation
- confidence
- influencing factors

---

# Alert

Alerts identify significant changes.

Examples include:

- approaching deadline
- ownership change
- legal update
- conflict detected

Alerts should remain actionable.

---

# Insight

Insights summarize patterns.

Examples include:

- portfolio analysis
- market trends
- customer behavior
- agency performance

Insights support strategic decisions.

---

# Prediction

Predictions estimate possible future outcomes.

Examples include:

- registration probability
- renewal probability
- conversion probability
- portfolio growth

Predictions should improve continuously through feedback.

---

# Intelligence Products

Intelligence publishes standardized Intelligence Products.

Examples include:

- Recommendation Feed
- Risk Dashboard
- Alert Feed
- Marketing Intelligence
- Brand Intelligence
- Portfolio Intelligence
- Customer Intelligence

Applications consume Intelligence Products.

Applications never consume internal reasoning.

---

# Marketing Intelligence

Marketing Intelligence discovers commercial opportunities.

Examples include:

- overseas filing demand
- portfolio expansion
- trademark acquisition
- renewal opportunities
- potential agency customers

Marketing Intelligence supports business growth.

---

# Brand Intelligence

Brand Intelligence evaluates brand protection.

Examples include:

- protection coverage
- portfolio completeness
- country coverage
- class coverage
- strategic gaps

Brand Intelligence connects legal rights with commercial value.

---

# Portfolio Intelligence

Portfolio Intelligence evaluates collections of rights.

Examples include:

- renewal planning
- geographical distribution
- unused assets
- licensing potential

Portfolio Intelligence supports long-term management.

---

# Customer Intelligence

Customer Intelligence supports customer development.

Examples include:

- customer value
- growth potential
- recommended services
- next best action

Customer Intelligence belongs to business support.

---

# Feedback

Every professional decision generates feedback.

Feedback may include:

- business outcome
- customer response
- attorney review
- workflow result
- execution quality

Feedback continuously improves future judgment.

Feedback never changes historical facts.

---

# Intelligence and Data

Data answers:

What is true?

Intelligence never changes facts.

Intelligence evaluates facts.

---

# Intelligence and Brain

Brain explains.

Intelligence judges.

Brain remains objective.

Intelligence becomes contextual.

---

# Intelligence and Capability

Intelligence recommends.

Capability performs.

Judgment precedes execution.

---

# Intelligence and Guide

Guide consumes Intelligence Products.

Guide decides:

- what to ask
- what to recommend
- which capability to invoke

Guide remains conversational.

Intelligence remains analytical.

---

# Intelligence Independence

Intelligence is independent of:

- AI models
- machine learning frameworks
- reasoning engines

Implementations may evolve.

Professional judgment should remain stable.

---

# Design Principles

Knowledge before judgment.

Signals before reasoning.

Reasoning before recommendation.

Recommendation before execution.

Feedback before improvement.

Explainability before automation.

Professional consistency before AI creativity.

---

# Summary

Intelligence is the professional judgment system of MarkOrbit.

It continuously transforms knowledge into reusable professional judgment.

Judgment becomes Intelligence Objects.

Intelligence Objects become Intelligence Products.

These products guide professional decisions while continuously improving through feedback.

Professional judgment should evolve together with the Trademark Universe.
