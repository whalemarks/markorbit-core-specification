# Purpose

Facts alone cannot solve professional problems.

Professional work requires structured knowledge.

The purpose of Brain is to continuously transform facts and professional experience into reusable knowledge.

Brain answers one question.

> **What does this mean?**

---

# Brain Philosophy

Reality exists.

Data describes reality.

Brain explains reality.

Knowledge is not information.

Knowledge is structured understanding.

Professional knowledge should continuously accumulate.

It should never disappear with individuals or organizations.

---

# The Knowledge Pipeline

Brain continuously transforms information into professional knowledge.

```
Knowledge Sources

↓

Brain Raw

↓

Knowledge Compilation

↓

Brain Objects

↓

Knowledge Graph

↓

Knowledge Products
```

Each layer has one responsibility.

---

# Knowledge Sources

Knowledge originates from many different sources.

Examples include:

Official Sources

- Trademark Laws
- Examination Guidelines
- Office Manuals
- International Treaties

Professional Sources

- Attorney Experience
- Internal Best Practices
- Professional Research
- Expert Opinions

Business Sources

- Customer Cases
- Office Actions
- Opposition Cases
- Renewal Cases

Operational Sources

- Emails
- Meeting Notes
- Documents
- Training Materials

Knowledge Sources provide evidence.

They are not knowledge.

---

# Brain Raw

Brain Raw preserves original knowledge materials.

Examples include:

- PDF
- Word
- Markdown
- Email
- Image
- Video Transcript
- Website

Brain Raw remains immutable.

It preserves evidence for future compilation.

---

# Knowledge Compilation

Compilation is the core responsibility of Brain.

Compilation transforms information into reusable knowledge.

Compilation may include:

- extraction
- normalization
- summarization
- comparison
- contradiction detection
- relationship discovery
- source attribution

Compilation is continuous.

Brain never recompiles everything.

Knowledge evolves incrementally.

---

# Brain Objects

Brain Objects are the smallest reusable knowledge units.

Examples include:

- Wiki
- Rule
- Decision
- Best Practice
- Template
- Explanation

Every Brain Object explains one professional concept.

Brain Objects never represent reality.

They represent understanding.

---

# Wiki

Wiki is the primary knowledge representation.

One Wiki explains one concept.

Examples include:

- Trademark Assignment
- Madrid Protocol
- US Section 8
- Nice Classification
- Likelihood of Confusion

One concept.

One Wiki.

---

# Rules

Rules describe repeatable professional logic.

Examples include:

- Time Limit Rules
- Priority Rules
- Applicant Requirements
- Renewal Rules

Rules should become machine-readable whenever practical.

---

# Decisions

Some professional knowledge cannot be expressed through rules.

Instead it becomes professional decisions.

Examples include:

- Filing Strategy
- Risk Evaluation
- Country Selection
- Protection Strategy

Decisions should preserve reasoning.

Not only conclusions.

---

# Best Practices

Best Practices preserve accumulated professional experience.

Examples include:

- Office Action Strategy
- Client Communication
- Portfolio Management
- Evidence Preparation

Best Practices evolve continuously.

---

# Templates

Templates provide reusable professional outputs.

Examples include:

- Email
- Proposal
- Office Action Reply
- POA
- Customer Notice

Templates reference Brain Objects.

Templates never replace Brain Objects.

---

# Knowledge Graph

Brain Objects are interconnected.

Knowledge therefore forms a graph.

Example

```
Trademark

↓

Likelihood of Confusion

↓

US Examination Practice

↓

Office Action

↓

Response Strategy
```

Knowledge Graph enables reasoning across professional concepts.

---

# Knowledge Products

Brain publishes standardized Knowledge Products.

Examples include:

- Wiki Library
- Rule Library
- Decision Library
- Template Library
- Best Practice Library

Applications consume Knowledge Products.

They do not consume Brain internals.

---

# Multiple Sources

One Brain Object may reference many sources.

Example

US Section 8

↓

USPTO Manual

↓

Trademark Act

↓

Attorney Experience

↓

Customer Cases

↓

Internal Practice

Different sources may disagree.

Brain preserves disagreement.

Brain records:

- source
- version
- timestamp
- confidence
- applicability

Professional knowledge becomes richer through diversity.

---

# Knowledge Evolution

Knowledge continuously evolves.

Brain Objects may be:

Created

↓

Expanded

↓

Merged

↓

Split

↓

Deprecated

↓

Archived

History should never disappear.

Every version remains traceable.

---

# Brain and Data

Data answers:

What is true?

Brain answers:

What does it mean?

Brain references Data.

Brain never changes Data.

---

# Brain and Intelligence

Brain provides understanding.

Intelligence provides judgment.

Brain remains objective.

Intelligence becomes contextual.

---

# Brain and Capability

Brain produces professional knowledge.

Capability operationalizes professional knowledge.

Every Capability should reference Brain Objects.

Brain remains the source of truth.

---

# Brain and Guide

Guide retrieves knowledge.

Guide never owns knowledge.

Guide combines:

- Knowledge Products
- Workspace Context
- User Intent

Guide explains professional concepts through Brain.

---

# Brain Independence

Brain is independent of:

- AI models
- vector databases
- search engines
- storage technologies

Implementation may change.

Knowledge should remain stable.

---

# Design Principles

Evidence before knowledge.

Knowledge before judgment.

Compile before retrieval.

Reference before duplication.

Evolution before overwrite.

Professional consistency before AI creativity.

---

# Summary

Brain is the professional knowledge system of MarkOrbit.

It continuously compiles information into reusable knowledge.

Knowledge becomes Brain Objects.

Brain Objects become Knowledge Products.

Knowledge Products power Intelligence, Capability, Guide and every future MarkOrbit product.

Professional knowledge should accumulate forever.
