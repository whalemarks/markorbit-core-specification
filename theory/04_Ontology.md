# Purpose

An operating system requires a common language.

Without a shared language:

- systems cannot communicate
- knowledge cannot accumulate
- capabilities cannot be reused
- artificial intelligence cannot reason consistently

The purpose of this document is to define the common ontology of MarkOrbit.

This ontology becomes the language shared by every repository, service, product, API, workflow and AI agent.

---

# Ontology

Ontology defines concepts.

Concepts define meaning.

Meaning enables communication.

Communication enables collaboration.

Collaboration enables evolution.

MarkOrbit therefore treats ontology as permanent infrastructure.

---

# Two Ontologies

MarkOrbit distinguishes two complementary ontologies.

```
Reality Ontology

↓

System Ontology
```

They describe different worlds.

Neither should replace the other.

---

# Reality Ontology

Reality Ontology describes the Trademark Universe.

It answers:

> What exists?

Reality concepts exist independently of software.

Examples include:

- Trademark
- Brand
- Owner
- Applicant
- Customer
- Agency
- Attorney
- Document
- Certificate
- Country
- Goods
- Event
- Relationship

Reality defines what the system represents.

---

# System Ontology

System Ontology describes the MarkOrbit Operating System.

It answers:

> How is reality represented and operated?

Examples include:

- Entity
- Data
- Brain
- Intelligence
- Capability
- Guide
- Workflow
- Workspace

System concepts operate reality.

They do not replace reality.

---

# Naming Principles

Every permanent concept has exactly one official definition.

The same concept should never have multiple meanings.

Different concepts should never share one definition.

Consistency is more valuable than convenience.

---

# Definition Standard

Every ontology entry should contain:

- Name
- Definition
- Category
- Responsibility
- Related Concepts

Future Registry implementations may include:

- Version
- Owner
- References
- Aliases

---

# Categories

Reality Ontology consists of five fundamental categories.

```
Actor

Asset

Event

Relationship

Ecosystem
```

Every Reality concept belongs to one primary category.

---

# Actors

Actors participate in the Trademark Universe.

Examples include:

- Applicant
- Owner
- Customer
- Agency
- Attorney
- Examiner

Actors perform actions.

Actors establish relationships.

Actors own assets.

---

# Assets

Assets possess legal or commercial value.

Examples include:

- Trademark
- Brand
- Portfolio
- Certificate
- Domain Name

Assets may evolve.

Assets may be transferred.

Assets may generate value.

---

# Events

Events describe change.

Examples include:

- Filing
- Publication
- Registration
- Assignment
- Renewal
- Cancellation

Events produce history.

---

# Relationships

Relationships connect reality.

Examples include:

Owner owns Trademark.

Trademark belongs to Brand.

Agency represents Owner.

Customer manages Portfolio.

Relationships are first-class concepts.

They should never be reduced to simple attributes.

---

# Ecosystem

The Trademark Universe exists within a broader environment.

Examples include:

- Trademark Offices
- Courts
- Laws
- Regulations
- Examination Guidelines
- International Treaties

The ecosystem continuously evolves.

---

---

# Concept Ownership

Every permanent concept owns exactly one responsibility.

| Concept | Responsibility |
|----------|----------------|
| Entity | Digital Representation |
| Data | Facts |
| Brain | Knowledge |
| Intelligence | Judgment |
| Capability | Professional Ability |
| Guide | Context & Interaction |
| Workflow | Business Execution |
| Workspace | Organizational Operation |

Responsibilities should never overlap.

---

Architecture follows ontology.

---

# Evolution

Ontology should evolve more slowly than implementation.

Definitions may become more precise.

Definitions should rarely change meaning.

Backward compatibility should always be considered.

---

# Registry

This document defines the ontology.

The ontology registry manages it.

Future versions of MarkOrbit will maintain a centralized ontology registry as the single source of truth for all permanent concepts.

---

# Design Principles

Language before implementation.

Definition before naming.

Consistency before convenience.

Reality before abstraction.

Ontology before database.

---

# Summary

Ontology is the common language of MarkOrbit.

Reality Ontology describes the Trademark Universe.

System Ontology describes the MarkOrbit Operating System.

Together they establish a shared language that enables consistent architecture, reusable capability and continuous evolution.

Every permanent concept within MarkOrbit should begin with ontology before implementation.
