# Purpose

MarkOrbit Core belongs to the entire Trademark Universe.

Organizations require their own operational environments.

The purpose of Workspace is to provide an independent operating space for every organization while sharing the same global infrastructure.

Workspace answers one question.

> **How does an organization operate on top of MarkOrbit Core?**

---

# Workspace Philosophy

Core is global.

Workspace is local.

Core defines professional standards.

Workspace defines organizational operation.

Core accumulates professional capability.

Workspace creates business value.

---

# The Operational Architecture

Every organization operates through its own Workspace.

```
MarkOrbit Core

↓

Workspace

↓

Products

↓

Users
```

The Core remains shared.

Each Workspace remains independent.

---

# What Is a Workspace

A Workspace is the operational environment of an organization.

It combines:

- organizational configuration
- local business
- local resources
- local workflows
- local intelligence
- local collaboration

Workspace is not a copy of MarkOrbit Core.

Workspace is an extension of MarkOrbit Core.

---

# Core Responsibilities

Workspace owns exactly one responsibility.

**Organizational Operation**

Workspace does not own:

- reality
- professional knowledge
- professional judgment
- professional capability

These belong to MarkOrbit Core.

---

# Workspace Components

A Workspace may contain:

- Members
- Roles
- Permissions
- Configuration
- Local Entities
- Local Documents
- Local Workflows
- Local Dashboards
- Local Reports
- Integrations

These components exist only within the Workspace.

---

# Global and Local

Workspace distinguishes between global and local resources.

## Global Resources

Provided by MarkOrbit Core.

Examples include:

- Global Entities
- Data Products
- Knowledge Products
- Intelligence Products
- Capability Catalog

Global resources are shared.

---

## Local Resources

Owned by the Workspace.

Examples include:

- Sales Leads
- Internal Notes
- Customer Preferences
- Local Documents
- Internal Reports
- Business Policies

Local resources belong only to one Workspace.

---

# Entity Mirror

Workspace may create mirrors of Global Entities.

```
Global Entity

↓

Workspace Mirror

↓

Local Context

↓

Business Operation
```

The mirror references the original Entity.

The original Entity remains the single source of truth.

Workspace never changes the Core definition.

---

# Organizational Configuration

Workspace configures how an organization operates.

Examples include:

- languages
- regions
- approval policies
- pricing rules
- supported services
- notification rules
- capability availability

Configuration changes behavior.

It never changes professional definitions.

---

# Members and Roles

Workspace manages organizational collaboration.

Examples include:

- Administrator
- Sales
- Attorney
- Trademark Specialist
- Finance
- Customer Service

Permissions belong to Workspace.

Professional capability belongs to Core.

---

# Local Intelligence

Workspace may generate organization-specific intelligence.

Examples include:

- sales performance
- customer activity
- renewal conversion
- team productivity
- regional opportunities

Local Intelligence complements Core Intelligence.

It never replaces it.

---

# Local Workflow

Workspace may configure local business workflows.

Examples include:

- approval processes
- sales processes
- customer onboarding
- document review

These workflows organize organizational operation.

They do not redefine Core Workflows.

---

# Guide in Workspace

Guide adapts according to Workspace configuration.

Workspace may configure:

- available capabilities
- interaction language
- service policies
- organizational terminology

Guide remains consistent while respecting organizational differences.

---

# Capability in Workspace

Workspace enables or disables Capabilities according to:

- subscriptions
- permissions
- regional compliance
- organization policies

Capability definitions remain unchanged.

Workspace controls availability.

---

# Shared Collaboration

Multiple Workspaces may collaborate.

Examples include:

- agency partnerships
- customer collaboration
- attorney cooperation
- enterprise groups

Collaboration occurs through shared references.

Ownership remains independent.

---

# Workspace Independence

Every Workspace operates independently.

Workspace data should remain isolated.

Core resources remain shared.

Organizations should never interfere with each other's operations.

---

# Evolution

Workspace continuously evolves.

Organizations grow.

Teams change.

Processes improve.

Configuration adapts.

The Core remains stable.

Workspace evolves around it.

---

# Design Principles

Core before Workspace.

Reference before copy.

Configuration before customization.

Isolation before interference.

Collaboration before duplication.

Business operation before implementation.

---

# Summary

Workspace is the organizational operating space of MarkOrbit.

It enables organizations to operate independently while sharing the same global professional infrastructure.

Core provides professional capability.

Workspace creates organizational value.

Together they allow the Trademark Universe to scale from individual professionals to global ecosystems without sacrificing consistency.
