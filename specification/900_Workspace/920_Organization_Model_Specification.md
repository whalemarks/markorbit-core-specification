# Purpose

Every Workspace operates within a professional organization.

The purpose of Organization Model is to define how professional responsibilities, authority and governance are organized.

Organization Model answers one question.

> **How does this organization professionally operate?**

---

# Organization Philosophy

Organizations are not collections of departments.

Organizations are systems of professional responsibilities.

Professional capabilities remain universal.

Organizations determine how those capabilities are governed.

---

# Scope

Organization Models describe reusable professional organizational structures.

Examples include:

- Trademark Agency Organization
- IP Law Firm Organization
- Corporate Legal Organization
- Marketplace Organization
- Government Organization
- Service Provider Organization

Organization Models remain independent of implementation.

---

# Responsibilities

Organization Model owns:

- organizational structure
- professional responsibilities
- authority model
- governance model
- operational policies
- collaboration model

Organization Model does not own:

- knowledge
- reasoning
- execution
- workflow implementation
- user interfaces

---

# Authority

- delegation
- ownership

# Design Principles

Responsibilities before Departments.

Authority before Hierarchy.

Governance before Implementation.

Professional Organization before Business Structure.

Policies before Technology.

Organization before Automation.

---

# Summary

Organization Model defines the reusable professional governance structure of a Workspace.

It organizes professional roles, responsibilities, authority and governance into configurable organizational models while preserving the universality of the Professional Operating System.

Organization Models allow different organizations to operate with distinct governance structures while sharing one common professional architecture.
