# Purpose

Professional experiences rarely consist of one isolated interaction.

Meaningful user value emerges when multiple interactions form a coherent experience.

The purpose of Experience Network is to organize Guide Objects into reusable professional experience networks.

Experience Network answers one question.

> **How do professional interactions become a complete professional experience?**

---

# Experience Philosophy

Guide Objects provide interactions.

Interaction Models define methodology.

Experience Networks organize continuity.

Workflow organizes execution.

Experience Networks organize user experience.

Not execution.

---

# Scope

Experience Networks describe reusable professional experience structures.

Examples include:

- Trademark Filing Experience

- Renewal Experience

- Portfolio Management Experience

- Brand Protection Experience

- Office Action Experience

- Customer Onboarding Experience

Experience Networks remain channel-independent.

---

# Responsibilities

Experience Network owns:

- experience continuity

- interaction collaboration

- navigation structure

- experience transitions

- user context continuity

Experience Network does not own:

- workflow

- execution

- reasoning

- persistence

---

# Architecture

Every Experience Network follows one structure.

```text
Guide Objects

↓

Interaction Models

↓

Experience Network

↓

Guide Product
```

Experience Networks organize professional experiences.

---

# Characteristics

Every Experience Network should satisfy:

## Continuous

Interactions should feel connected.

---

## Context-preserving

User context should remain consistent.

---

## Adaptive

Different users may experience different paths.

---

## Explainable

Users should always understand where they are.

---

## Channel-independent

Experience should survive channel switching.

---

## Versioned

Experience evolves independently.

# Context Continuity

Experience Networks preserve:

- user intent

- current objective

- previous interactions

- completed work

- recommended next actions

Context continuity reduces cognitive load.

---

# Navigation

Navigation should support:

- continue

- revisit

- compare

- branch

- complete

Navigation serves experience.

Not execution.

---

# Validation

Validation should verify:

- disconnected experiences

- broken transitions

- inconsistent context

- missing explanations

- accessibility

---

# Runtime

Runtime renders Experience Network Sessions.

Definitions remain immutable.

Runtime tracks experience state.

---

# Relationship to Workflow

Experience Network defines:

How users experience work.

Workflow defines:

How organizations perform work.

The two should remain independent.

---

# Relationship to Workspace

Workspace customizes:

- branding

- localization

- permissions

- personalization

Core experience remains reusable.

---

# Compatibility

Experience Networks evolve conservatively.

Experience Contracts remain stable.

Breaking experience semantics require major versions.

---

# Extension

Future Experience Networks may include:

- Voice Experience

- Mixed Reality Experience

- Human-AI Collaboration Experience

- Autonomous Agent Experience

Extensions should preserve continuity.

---

# Non Goals

Experience Network is not:

- Workflow

- User Journey

- Navigation Tree

- UI Flow

- Site Map

These may implement Experience Networks.

They are not Experience Networks.

Continuity before Navigation.

---

# Summary

Experience Network defines how professional interactions are connected into complete professional experiences within MarkOrbit.

It organizes Guide Objects and Interaction Models into adaptive, explainable and channel-independent experience structures.

Experience Networks enable users to move seamlessly across professional services while preserving context, trust and continuity, regardless of interface or execution environment.
