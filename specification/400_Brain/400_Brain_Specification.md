# Purpose

Persistence preserves facts.

Facts alone do not solve professional problems.

Professionals interpret facts using accumulated knowledge.

The purpose of Brain is to transform trusted information into reusable professional understanding.

Brain answers one question.

> **What do these facts mean?**

---

# Brain Philosophy

Facts are objective.

Understanding is structured.

Judgment is contextual.

Brain never changes facts.

Brain explains facts.

Brain organizes facts.

Brain connects facts.

Brain is the semantic understanding layer.

---

# Scope

Brain includes every permanent professional understanding model.

Examples include:

- Trademark Examination Rules
- Nice Classification Knowledge
- Office Practices
- Filing Strategies
- Opposition Principles
- Renewal Rules
- Country Knowledge
- Legal Interpretation
- Brand Protection Methodology

Brain does not make recommendations.

Brain prepares knowledge.

---

# Responsibilities

Brain owns:

- professional concepts
- semantic understanding
- knowledge organization
- knowledge relationships
- reusable expertise

Brain does not own:

- facts
- recommendations
- execution
- workflows

---

# Brain Architecture

Brain follows one architecture.

```text
Persistence Products

↓

Brain Objects

↓

Knowledge Model

↓

Knowledge Graph

↓

Knowledge Products
```

Every layer increases semantic understanding.

---

# Brain Characteristics

Every Brain component should satisfy:

## Explainable

Knowledge should explain why.

---

## Reusable

Knowledge should support many Capabilities.

---

## Traceable

Knowledge should preserve lineage back to Products.

---

## Structured

Knowledge should be machine-readable.

---

## Stable

Knowledge evolves slowly.

Interpretations should remain versioned.

---

# Brain Pipeline

Brain transforms information.

```text
Facts

↓

Concepts

↓

Relationships

↓

Knowledge

↓

Published Knowledge
```

Brain creates professional understanding.

---

# Brain and Persistence

Brain consumes Products.

Brain should not depend directly on Stores.

Products remain the official knowledge input.

---

# Brain and Intelligence

Brain provides understanding.

Intelligence performs reasoning.

Brain says:

> "This is the examination rule."

Intelligence says:

> "This application will probably receive an Office Action."

---

# Brain and Capability

Capabilities consume Brain.

Capabilities should never hardcode professional knowledge.

Knowledge belongs to Brain.

---

# Brain and Guide

Guide explains Brain.

Guide translates professional knowledge into user language.

Brain remains professional.

---

# Brain and Workflow

Workflow references Brain.

Workflow should not embed legal knowledge.

Professional understanding belongs to Brain.

---

# Knowledge Evolution

Knowledge evolves.

Examples:

```text
Office Practice

↓

Updated

↓

Brain Version 2.1
```

Historical knowledge remains available.

---

# Validation

Brain should validate:

- source lineage
- semantic consistency
- ontology compliance
- relationship integrity
- publication readiness

---

# Compatibility

Brain evolves independently.

Products remain referenceable.

Capabilities remain compatible through Contracts.

---

# Extension

Future Brain extensions may include:

- Industry Brain
- Country Brain
- Litigation Brain
- AI-assisted Brain
- Community Brain

Extensions preserve semantic consistency.

---

# Non Goals

Brain is not:

- Database
- AI Model
- Chatbot
- Workflow
- Recommendation Engine

Brain represents professional understanding.

Nothing more.

---

# Design Principles

Facts before Knowledge.

Knowledge before Judgment.

Understanding before Recommendation.

Semantics before Algorithms.

Explanation before Automation.

Lineage before Convenience.

---

# Summary

Brain is the professional understanding layer of MarkOrbit.

It transforms trusted information into structured knowledge that can be reused throughout the Operating System.

Brain bridges Persistence and Intelligence.

It explains facts.

It does not judge them.

Professional understanding is the foundation upon which all intelligent behavior is built.
